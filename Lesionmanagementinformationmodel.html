Lesion management information model

The clinical data model used by the AIR module (Fig. 1) consists of two types of FHIR resources that capture the features of the findings of AI models, and the input of the radiologist who is assessing them.

  • AIRBodyStructure profile: a specialization of the ILS BodyStructure profile intended to capture the anatomical location (.location) and the type (.morphology) of a lesion. It allows cross-referencing of the same lesion between imaging modalities, and potentially also other types of non-imaging studies.

  • Observation resources: three types of profiles capture de radiological features of the lesion

    • AIRObservationRoot: this profile intermediates between the lesion (BodyStructure) through .focus and the following individual findings through .hasmember. It has a fixed .code:"http://snomed.info/sct"|"404684003"|"Clinical finding", and a .value.codeableconcept and .bodysite that match AIRBodyStructure.morphology and AIRBodystructure.location respectively. It also holds the references to other components of the Health Information System, namely:
      • Patient: represents the person from who the images were taken.
      • ImagingStudy: provides information on a DICOM imaging study.
      • Device: represents an instance of the algorithm that outputs the findings in the study.
      • ServiceRequest: provides the record of an order for a service to be performed that would result in an imaging study.
      • Practitioner: represents the radiologist that interacts with the AIR system accepting, rejecting, or enriching the findings.
    • AIRObservationQuantitative captures the quantitative measurements of the lesion.
    • Qualitative measurements are captured in a specific profile for each type of feature. The .code element defines the type of feature observed as a codeable concept, and .valueCodeableConcept the value within that domain. Two example profiles from a lung nodule use case in thorax CT scan are included in this implementation guide as a reference: AIRObservationSolidness and AIRObservationMargins
